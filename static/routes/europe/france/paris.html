Paris business class guide

Paris rewards a slower pace: choose a few major monuments, one museum-heavy day, and time for neighbourhood cafés, river views, and evening walks.

ParisFranceAirports: CDG, LBG, ORY

What to do in Paris

Use this city page to plan the ground side of the trip first, then choose the longer business class sectors that are genuinely worth flying in a lie-flat seat.

  • Pair the Eiffel Tower or Trocadéro with a Seine cruise for the classic first-day overview.
  • Use the Louvre, Musée d’Orsay, or smaller Left Bank museums depending on how museum-heavy you want the trip to feel.
  • Walk through Saint-Germain, Le Marais, or the Champs-Élysées corridor for shopping, cafés, and late-night city energy.

Where to focus your time

Paris is strongest when you choose one base and a clear pace for the stay instead of trying to cover the entire city in a rush.

  • Le Marais for boutiques and historic streets.
  • Saint-Germain-des-Prés for galleries, cafés, and polished hotels.
  • The 8th arrondissement for classic luxury addresses and flagship shopping.

Best for: romantic trips, art weekends, shopping, and high-end hotel stays.
