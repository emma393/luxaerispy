What to do in San Francisco

Use this city page to plan the ground side of the trip first, then choose the longer business class sectors that are genuinely worth flying in a lie-flat seat.

  • Pair the waterfront with one classic view or ferry-based outing.
  • Use North Beach, Chinatown, or the Ferry Building for food-heavy wandering.
  • Keep a half-day for parks or bridge views if the weather is clear.

Where to focus your time

San Francisco is strongest when you choose one base and a clear pace for the stay instead of trying to cover the entire city in a rush.

  • Embarcadero / Financial District for convenience.
  • Nob Hill for classic hotels.
  • Hayes Valley for dining and boutiques.

Best for: scenic city breaks, food, and California multi-stop trips.
