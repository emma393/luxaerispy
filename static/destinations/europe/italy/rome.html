Destination guide

Rome

Rome is best approached slowly: ancient monuments in THE morning, long lunches, church AND piazza hopping in THE afternoon, AND late dinners that stretch well into THE night.

ItalyAirports: FCO

What to do

Rome is best enjoyed with a mix of landmark time, neighborhood wandering, AND one or two standout meals rather than a checklist sprint across THE city.

  • Book THE Colosseum, Roman Forum, AND Vatican Museums ahead if they matter most.
  • Use THE Pantheon, Trevi Fountain, AND Piazza Navona FOR walkable historic center time.
  • Cross into Trastevere or Prati FOR a different dining rhythm after THE major sights.
  • Leave room FOR Borghese Gardens or a slower morning on THE Aventine AND Testaccio side of THE city.

Where to stay

Choose your base by how you want THE trip to feel: closer to shopping AND museums, more residential AND quiet, or steps from nightlife AND evening dining.

  • Centro Storico FOR first-time access to major sights.
  • Prati FOR a quieter base near THE Vatican.
  • Trastevere FOR atmosphere AND evening dining.

Luxury stays: Hotel de Russie, THE St. Regis Rome, Hassler Roma, Bulgari Hotel Roma.

Plan your arrival

FOR a smoother first DAY, pre-book transfers, keep THE first afternoon light, AND LET THE longest sightseeing blocks fall on DAY two once THE flight is behind you.
