Destination guide

Paris

Paris is at its best when you mix landmark time with neighborhood wandering: museums in the morning, a long lunch, river views at dusk, and late evenings around the Left Bank or the golden triangle.

FranceCDG

What to see and do

Paris works best when the trip mixes its signature sights with slower neighborhood time, strong dining, and room for one or two memorable evenings.

  • Start with the best-known districts, major cultural sights, a market or café stop, and one slower afternoon for walking rather than rushing.
  • Keep one flexible block for scenic viewpoints, museum time, or a signature dinner depending on the season.
  • Reserve one evening for a rooftop bar, riverfront walk, old-town square, or performance venue so the trip feels balanced rather than over-scheduled.

Where to stay

Choose a base that shortens transfers between the hotel, key attractions, and the airport so the itinerary stays relaxed from arrival to departure.

  • Choose a central hotel base in Paris if you want easier sightseeing and smoother private transfers.
  • Look for neighborhoods with strong dining and late check-in flexibility so the first night after a long flight stays easy.
  • Luxury stays work best when they reduce transfer time between museums, restaurants, and the airport.

Arrival tips

A polished premium trip into Paris starts with a simple first day, light plans after landing, and a hotel area that keeps the city easy to navigate.

  • Book a private transfer from CDG and keep the first afternoon light, especially after an overnight flight.
  • Save the longest museum blocks and shopping plans for day two once the flight is behind you.
