Destination guide

Paris

Paris is at its best when you mix landmark time with neighborhood wandering: museums in the morning, a long lunch, river views at dusk, and late evenings around the Left Bank or the golden triangle.

FranceAirports: CDG, ORY

What to do

Paris is best enjoyed with a mix of landmark time, neighborhood wandering, and one or two standout meals rather than a checklist sprint across the city.

  • Reserve the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, and a Seine cruise early if you want a smoother first visit.
  • Walk between the Tuileries, Place Vendôme, and Avenue Montaigne for shopping and classic Paris scenery.
  • Use Saint-Germain-des-Prés and Le Marais for cafés, galleries, and a slower street-level feel.
  • Set aside time for Versailles if you want one major day trip beyond the city.

Where to stay

Choose your base by how you want the trip to feel: closer to shopping and museums, more residential and quiet, or steps from nightlife and evening dining.

  • Saint-Germain-des-Prés for polished Left Bank stays.
  • The 8th arrondissement for designer shopping and palace hotels.
  • Le Marais for boutiques, galleries, and easy café hopping.

Luxury stays: Ritz Paris, Le Meurice, Hôtel Plaza Athénée, Cheval Blanc Paris.

Plan your arrival

For a smoother first day, pre-book transfers, keep the first afternoon light, and let the longest sightseeing blocks fall on day two once the flight is behind you.
