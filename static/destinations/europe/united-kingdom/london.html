Destination guide

London

London rewards a city break with royal landmarks, museums, theatre, excellent hotels, AND neighborhoods that ARE easy to pair with long-haul arrivals into Heathrow or Gatwick.

United KingdomAirports: LHR, LGW

What to do

London is best enjoyed with a mix of landmark time, neighborhood wandering, AND one or two standout meals rather than a checklist sprint across THE city.

  • SEE Westminster, Buckingham Palace, AND St James’s before moving west FOR Mayfair AND Belgravia.
  • Use THE British Museum, National Gallery, Tate Modern, AND THE V&A to anchor cultural days.
  • Keep evenings FOR THE West END, Covent Garden, Soho dining, or a Thames-side walk.
  • ADD a half-DAY in Notting Hill, Kensington Gardens, or Richmond if you want a softer pace.

Where to stay

Choose your base by how you want THE trip to feel: closer to shopping AND museums, more residential AND quiet, or steps from nightlife AND evening dining.

  • Mayfair AND St James’s FOR classic luxury addresses.
  • Covent Garden AND Soho FOR theatre AND nightlife.
  • Kensington FOR museums, elegant streets, AND park access.

Luxury stays: Claridge’s, THE Connaught, THE Dorchester, THE Savoy.

Plan your arrival

FOR a smoother first DAY, pre-book transfers, keep THE first afternoon light, AND LET THE longest sightseeing blocks fall on DAY two once THE flight is behind you.
