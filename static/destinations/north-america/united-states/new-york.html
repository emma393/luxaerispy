Destination guide

New York

New York is a city for museums, skyline views, Broadway nights, serious shopping, and long weekends that can move from Central Park mornings to downtown dining without missing a beat.

United StatesAirports: JFK, LGA, EWR

What to do

New York is best enjoyed with a mix of landmark time, neighborhood wandering, and one or two standout meals rather than a checklist sprint across the city.

  • Walk Central Park and Fifth Avenue for classic Manhattan landmarks and flagship shopping.
  • Book Broadway or Lincoln Center for an evening built around theatre, opera, or ballet.
  • Use the High Line, Hudson Yards, and the waterfront for modern city views and easy daytime strolling.
  • Split museum time between The Met, MoMA, and the Guggenheim depending on your interests.

Where to stay

Choose your base by how you want the trip to feel: closer to shopping and museums, more residential and quiet, or steps from nightlife and evening dining.

  • Midtown for first-time stays close to theatre and shopping.
  • The Upper East Side for museums, quieter luxury hotels, and park access.
  • Downtown for Tribeca, Soho, and a more residential feel with strong dining.

Luxury stays: The Plaza, The St. Regis New York, Aman New York, Four Seasons Downtown.

Plan your arrival

For a smoother first day, pre-book transfers, keep the first afternoon light, and let the longest sightseeing blocks fall on day two once the flight is behind you.
