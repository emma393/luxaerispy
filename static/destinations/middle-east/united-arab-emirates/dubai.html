Destination guide

Dubai

Dubai blends landmark skyline views with beach clubs, desert escapes, ambitious dining, and some of the world’s most recognizable luxury hotels.

United Arab EmiratesAirports: DXB

What to do

Dubai is best enjoyed with a mix of landmark time, neighborhood wandering, and one or two standout meals rather than a checklist sprint across the city.

  • Start with Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall, and the fountain district before branching into DIFC and Downtown.
  • Use a desert experience, marina yacht charter, or beach day to break up the city pace.
  • Reserve time in Old Dubai if you want souks, creek crossings, and a more historic angle on the city.
  • Keep evenings for destination dining or a skyline lounge rather than trying to rush too much into one day.

Where to stay

Choose your base by how you want the trip to feel: closer to shopping and museums, more residential and quiet, or steps from nightlife and evening dining.

  • Downtown Dubai for Burj Khalifa and major hotels.
  • Jumeirah and the Palm for resort stays and beach time.
  • DIFC for dining and a polished business-travel base.

Luxury stays: Burj Al Arab, Atlantis The Royal, Armani Hotel Dubai, One&Only One Za’abeel.

Plan your arrival

For a smoother first day, pre-book transfers, keep the first afternoon light, and let the longest sightseeing blocks fall on day two once the flight is behind you.
