New York

New York works best when you balance big-name sights with neighbourhood time, standout meals, and one or two booking-heavy experiences such as Broadway or a skyline observation deck.

New YorkUnited StatesAirports: JFK, JRA, JRB, LGA

What to do in New York

  • Use Midtown for Broadway, classic skyline icons, and a polished first-day orientation.
  • Plan a Museum Mile or Downtown day depending on whether you care more about galleries or neighbourhood energy.
  • Add a harbour cruise, rooftop evening, or Brooklyn crossing for the city views people remember.

Where to base yourself

  • Midtown for easy landmark access.
  • SoHo and Tribeca for dining and boutique shopping.
  • Upper East Side for museum access and classic luxury hotels.

Good for: shopping, dining, culture, and short premium city breaks.
